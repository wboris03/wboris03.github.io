About

Research profile

My research is centered on optimal control theory and its applications to a broad class of dynamical systems. I develop and analyze frameworks that combine rigorous mathematical analysis with advanced computational methods, with the aim of addressing structurally rich control problems arising in geometry, quantum systems, and PDEs.

A recurring thread in my work is the search for methods that preserve relevant geometric or algebraic structure while remaining computationally effective. This perspective informs both the qualitative analysis of control systems and the design of efficient numerical algorithms.

Current positioning

I am currently building a coherent research agenda at the crossroads of optimal control, geometric methods, and scientific computing, with applications ranging from quantum control to PDE-constrained optimization.

Methodological approach

My work integrates first- and second-order optimality conditions, geometric analysis, structure-preserving discretization, direct and indirect numerical methods, and algorithmic design for nonlinear and high-dimensional systems.

Projects

Selected research themes

Geometric Optimal Control

Analysis of Zermelo-type problems, first- and second-order optimality conditions, and links with Finsler and Riemannian geometry.

Quantum Optimal Control

Development of Cayley-transform-based numerical methods for linear and nonlinear quantum control systems with emphasis on robustness and structure preservation.

Optimal Control of PDEs

Optimal control of mean curvature flows, necessary optimality conditions, and finite-element-based numerical approaches.

Turnpike Theory

Investigation of turnpike phenomena for continuous- and discrete-time control systems, especially in the presence of symmetries.

Numerical Algorithms for Control

Design of direct and indirect optimization algorithms for optimal control problems, with focus on reliability, efficiency, and mathematical consistency.

Future Direction

A long-term objective is to strengthen the bridge between geometric insight and high-performance computation in control problems governed by complex dynamical systems.
