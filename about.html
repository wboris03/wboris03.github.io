About

Research profile

My research is centered on optimal control theory and its applications to dynamical systems, with a combined analytical and numerical perspective. I work at the intersection of applied mathematics, geometric control, computational methods, and control-driven modelling.

My main research directions include geometric optimal control, quantum optimal control, PDE-constrained optimization, turnpike theory, and the development of efficient numerical algorithms. This combination allows me to move from theoretical optimality conditions to implementable computational schemes.

Methodological approach

I combine first- and second-order optimality theory, geometric analysis, structure-preserving integration, finite element methods, and direct or indirect optimization techniques.

Scientific identity

My work aims to build bridges between rigorous control theory and high-performance numerical practice, with a particular emphasis on systems with geometric structure or symmetry.
